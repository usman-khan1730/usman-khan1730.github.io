Engineering Projects

Some of the main engineering projects I have undertaken
during MEng Mechanical Engineering at UCL.

Sustainable Cellulose Foams

A novel method of producing sustainable cellulose foams, where lignocellulosic macrofibres were extracted from bamboo and sisal plant and cross-linked with cellulosic fibrils in order to manufacture polyurethane foam rivalling insulation panels with competitive thermal properties, lesser CO2 emissions, higher compressive strength and superior flame-resistance.

Expand Project

MR-Resistant Cardiac Catheterisation Robot

Novel design of an MR-Resistant Robot, which can perform cardiac catheterisation without exposing ferromagnetic materials to the MR zone. Implementing a master/slave pneumatic system, it applies 127.5mm linear actuation and continuous rotation to a balloon catheter through a fluid pipeline; with a unit cost of under £300 and operated by joysticks for ergonomics.

Expand Project

Turboprop Engine Impeller

Design and manufacture of a scaled-down turboprop engine impeller, aiming to improve efficiency. Mathematical modelling and CFD employed to minimise outflow misalignment, with CES used to optimise for material and manufacturing method. Final 3D printed impeller blends reliability and performance, whilst accounting for only 0.5% of total engine footprint.

Expand Project

Engineering Scenarios: Jet Engine Bracket & EV Battery Tray

Redesign of a titanium Jet Engine Bracket in Fusion 360. Generative design employed to reduce mass by 45%, whilst optimising minimum safety factors exhibited under extreme load conditions. 3-Axis CNC machining toolpath fashioned for its manufacture that is efficient and cost-effective.

Redesign of a swappable electric vehicle battery tray, using analytical calculations to improve bolt layout and CES Edupack to optimise material. New tray reduces mass by 25%, whilst exhibiting exceptionally low maximum stresses and vertical deflections under road-condition load cases.
